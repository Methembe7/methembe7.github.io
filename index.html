About

Hi! My name is Methembe, and I'm currently a Senior at LeMoyne-Owen College majoring in Computer Science.
I’m motivated by bridging gaps and creating opportunities. From studying in a small township in Bulawayo, Zimbabwe, to interning with different tech organizations in Memphis, Atlanta and New York, I’ve been blessed with a lot of opportunities.
Now, I want to use them to create products and movements that enable access to resources in all the communities I've had the chance to live in. And that’s how I know I want to be a Product Manager.

Feel free to navigate around this website to learn more about me, as well as my experience, passions and projects!

My Internships

7Park Data

This past summer, I interned with the Product team at 7Park Data, which is a New York based startup. The team's goal is to deliver the best products that allow investors and business leaders to make better data-driven decisions. My intern project involved working with data scientists, engineers, research analysts and a designer to build a Tableau dashboard which will be used by key Financial Service clients.
Having complete ownership over a product and leading its discovery, development and even setting up its QA pipeline was an exciting experience.

Mailchimp

In the summer of 2019, I interned with the Marketing Analytics team at Mailchimp in Atlanta. My internship was focused on analyzing user activity and evaluating influencers on the Mailchimp platforms and reporting key metrics to 7 product and marketing teams. I also led the redesign of the Google Tag Manager tag implementation pipeline, saving over $500K.
This experience helped me to discover my superpower- working in cross-functional teams and being the voice of the user to build the best products.

AutoZone

In the summer and fall of 2018, I interned with the IT Quality Assurance team at AutoZone in Memphis. Although I had just completed my freshman year, I had the huge task of testing all the e-commerce code from the company's Engineering teams. I was involved throughout the whole lifecycle of the products: from creating design specs to executing security and performance tests just before each launch.
Reporting directly to senior engineers and business leaders, this internship taught me the invaluable lesson of communication and creating quality products that customers love.

Projects

Partners In Crime

This was a 3-day hackathon project for the DubsTech Protothon. In the team 4, our goal was to solve the problem faced by police officers when they step out of their vehicles and have only their phones as a means of communicating with their stations and connecting.
As the UX Researcher, I conducted user interviews, identified pain points and pitched the problem statement. Through rapid prototyping and design thinking, my team and I designed interactive prototypes using Figma and presented our project to a panel of judges.
Created: Spring 2020

City Connect

This was a weekend-long project for the Memphis Civic Data Hackathon. Through this hackathon, I was able to use my passion for using technology to solve human problems. One of the biggest problems in Memphis is a very poorly designed and unreliable bus system.
My team and I tackled this problem by first collecting user feedback from the residents that use the bus across the city and we identified under-utilized bus stops using QGIS, pandas and Geopy. To solve the problem, we designed a new distribution of bus stops that would decrease the residents' wait time by 10%.
Created: Spring 2020

Emotional Chat

This project was made in one week in collaboration with three other students for the VandyHacks Hackathon. Emotional Chat is a webapp that allows people to genuinely communicate and express their feelings and reactions as they are chatting.
Emojis are an attempt to allow people to express their emotions but emojis rarely do justice in conveying the accurate reaction to the recepient of the message. Using the Microsoft FaceAPI and Twilio API, my team and I developed Emotional Chat to bridge this gap. As the UI Developer, I used React and Redux on the frontEnd.
Created: August 2017

Flixster

Flixster is an Android application that allows a user to view a list of the latests movies sourced from the The Movie Database API, their overviews, ratings and also to watch the movie trailers.
Using the YouTubePlayerView API, the user can watch each listed movie's trailer from YouTube on the app. Open-source libraries used: Movie Database API, YouTubePlayerView API, Android Async HTTP and Glide.

Leadership

Up 'a' Step

In 2019, I co-founded and served as the Chief Technology Officer of Up 'a' Step which provides a global community and network for more than 600 students to inspire each other and share positive content on personal and professional development.
The motivation came from a problem I experienced after transitioning to the educational system in the US as an international student. I struggled to find content online that was relatable to my experience as a student and witnessed a lot of my friends dropping out or switching programs due to a lack of motivation.
It allowed me to research and find ways to support students through content on social media platforms.

BDPA

On campus, I serve as the President of the Black Data Processors Associates. BDPA is a community of students on campus who are passionate about using tech to solve real human problems.
My motivation to build this community came from the problems I used to experience as a freshman. I struggled to connect with internships and to get mentors in technology. Through BDPA, we have since built a supportive community of more than 20 students and organized more than 10 meetups on campus with professionals and recruiters.

STS Elite

I’m motivated by bridging gaps and creating opportunities. One of the ways that I do this is through serving as the Membership Chair of STS Elite. STS Elite is the largest leadership and professional development organization in the city of Memphis.
As the Membership Chair, I am the liason between students and their mentors. I also confer with students and an advisory council to assess how to relate curriculum materials to the students’ individual needs

Blacks in Cyber

When I'm not in class, tutoring my CS students, working out or attending meetings, I enjoy learning about new and emerging technology! As the Campus Ambassador for Blacks In Cyber, I lead bi-weekly discussions, organize group projects and Capture the Flag competitions for more than 30 students on campus.
